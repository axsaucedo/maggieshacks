A HackDay to create revolutionary concepts to improve the life of cancer patients.

Your opportunity to make a difference.

Maggie's Hackathon is a 12-hour HackDay that aims to change the lives of cancer patients. Twenty of Britain's best developers and entrepreneurs will have a chance to hack innovative concepts together with Bloomberg employees, and revolutionize the lives of cancer patients!

The inspiration for Maggie's Hack was ignited through a project from Maggie's Centres, in which they envisioned a bunch of skillful and talented individuals, coming together to help them find new, innovative ways to deliver better online experiences to cancer patients.

Websites, apps and prototypes will be created, and will be presented in front of Maggie's experts, partners and senior Bloomberg employees. The winners will not only obtain great prizes, but they will have an opportunity to engage with Maggie's Centres and pitch their concept in front of the Maggie's core team.

Who was Maggie?

Maggie Keswick Jencks lived with advanced cancer for two years. During that time she used her knowledge and experience to create a blueprint for a new type of care. Maggie’s Centres are built around her belief that people should not “lose the joy of living in the fear of dying”.

The Challenge

Maggie's provides free practical, emotional and social support to people with cancer and their family and friends.

Maggie's is trying to enhance the experience of all their cancer patiences through the use of technology. However in order for them to achieve this, they require the help of innovative, skillful individuals - this is where you come in!

At Maggie's hackday, individuals will have an opportunity to hack for good - ideas will be formulated around technologies and concepts that Maggie's can use to enhance their patience's experiences. Solutions may include mobile applications, web applications, web plugins, etc.

All attendees will have access to Maggie's resources before the event so that they can optimize their solutions and manage to build a revolutionary prototype by the end of the HackDay.
